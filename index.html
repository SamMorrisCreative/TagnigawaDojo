BUJINKAN

The Bujinkan Organisation was formed by Sōke (Grandmaster) Masaaki Hatsumi in 1970, bringing the art of Bujinkan Budo Taijutsu to the world.

Though commonly referred to as Ninjutsu, Bujinkan Budo Taijutsu is actually a combat system composed of nine different schools of Japanese martial arts, with both Samurai and Ninja heritage, including armed and unarmed combat, evasion, tactics, horsemanship, and medicine.

Ninjutsu is not a martial art based on size, strength, or speed, but around a person’s own individual ability. There are no rules. There are no tournaments. This is not a sport. It is simply about how to win.

TANIGAWA DOJO

ANDY WHITE

Starting with Judo in 1963, Andy has gone on to practice a variety of martial arts including Kenjutsu, Wadō-ryū, Krav Maga, and Kickboxing, setting up the Forest Academy of Martial arts in 1995 to take on his own students. He took up Bujinkan in 2008 and earned his 6th Dan in 2018.

“It’s an opportunity to bring all these skills under one roof. Having 9 Schools to draw from gives you a vast wealth of techniques to learn and teach.”
- Andy on why he took up Bujinkan.
